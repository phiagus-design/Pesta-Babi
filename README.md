>source:Documentary: Pesta Babi and Book: Pendidikan Berkebudayaan
These sources examine the **systemic displacement and marginalization** of indigenous communities in **South Papua** caused by the Indonesian government’s large-scale **industrial food and energy projects**. The primary narrative details how massive **deforestation for rice, sugarcane, and palm oil** plantations—often guarded by the military—destroys local ecosystems and the cultural identity of tribes like the **Awyu, Marind, and Muyu**. Indigenous resistance is highlighted through symbolic acts such as the planting of **thousands of red crosses** and the preservation of traditional **pig feasts** as means of asserting land rights. Additionally, the text contrasts these realities with **Yudi Latif’s educational theories**, which argue that true development must respect **cultural values** rather than treating humans as mere industrial tools. Ultimately, the materials characterize the state's actions as a form of **modern colonialism** that prioritizes corporate profits over the survival of indigenous civilizations.
